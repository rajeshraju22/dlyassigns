Eagle is a common name for many large birds of prey of the family Accipitridae;
belongs to several groups of genera that are not necessarily closely
to each other.

Eagles are large, powerfully built birds of prey, with a heavy head and beak. Even the smallest eagles,
like the booted eagle (Aquila pennata) (which is comparable in size to a common buzzard (Buteo buteo)
or red-tailed hawk (B. jamaicensis)), have relatively longer and more evenly broad wings, and more direct,
than any other raptors apart from some vultures.
